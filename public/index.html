What FleetFinder does

FleetFinder tracks every vehicle in your fleet: its condition, service schedule, location, and a photo record of any damage. The dashboard you're looking at is the office view. Employees use their phones to check vehicles in.

Adding & managing vehicles

Click + Add vehicle in the Vehicles panel and fill in the name, make, model, plate, and status. A vehicle's row shows its status, location, odometer, last check-in, and next service due. Use Delete to remove a vehicle.

Check-ins & the inspection checklist

A check-in is a quick vehicle inspection. Click Check-in on a vehicle (or have an employee scan its QR code). They enter their name and complete a 7-step checklist — odometer, oil, tire rotation, wash, fluids, tire condition, and lights. The check-in can't be submitted until every item is done, so nothing gets skipped.

QR codes

Every vehicle has its own QR code. Use QR on a row to print one, or Print QR codes for a full sheet. Stick the code inside the cab. When an employee scans it with a phone, they land on that vehicle's service page and can run a check-in on the spot.

The access PIN

Phones connecting over Wi-Fi must enter a shared PIN the first time. This office dashboard (on the host Mac) never needs it. The PIN is set in the .env file in the FleetFinder folder.

Service schedule

From check-in history, FleetFinder works out when each vehicle is next due for an oil change, tire rotation, wash, and inspection. The Service due panel lists anything overdue or coming up. Each vehicle row also shows its next service as a colored tag.

Jobsite dispatch

When a vehicle leaves, click Send out (optionally note the driver). When it returns, click Mark returned. The Out on jobsites panel shows everything that's away and for how long.

Damage photos

Open a vehicle's Check-in window — or its phone page — and use the Damage photos section to upload pictures of any damage with a short description. A 📷 badge appears on the vehicle's row.

Reports

The buttons at the top right export spreadsheets (CSV) you can open in Excel, Numbers, or Google Sheets: Vehicles, Check-ins, Schedule, and Trips.
